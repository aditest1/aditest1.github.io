Flow Chart of Light Processing

Cornea
The cornea bends light as it enters the eye, providing a significant amount of the eye's focusing power.
→
Pupil
The pupil adjusts its size to regulate the amount of light entering the eye, expanding in low light and contracting in bright light.
→
Lens
The lens fine-tunes the focus of light onto the retina by changing its shape, allowing us to see objects at various distances.
→
Retina
The retina detects the focused light and converts it into electrical signals, which are then sent to the brain via the optic nerve.
→
Optic Nerve
The optic nerve transmits visual information from the retina to the brain, where it is interpreted as images.

Deficiency Chart

Cataract

Reason: Clouding of the eye's lens, often due to aging or injury.

Treatment: Surgical removal of the cloudy lens and replacement with an artificial lens.

Myopia (Nearsightedness)

Reason: Light is focused in front of the retina due to an elongated eyeball or overly curved lens.

Treatment: Corrective lenses (glasses or contact lenses) or refractive surgery.

Hyperopia (Farsightedness)

Reason: Light is focused behind the retina due to a shortened eyeball or a less curved lens.

Treatment: Corrective lenses (glasses or contact lenses) or refractive surgery.
